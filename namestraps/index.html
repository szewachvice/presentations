TVP World Namestrap Style

Arthur Barys

2025-03-18

A Namestrap:

  • Gives viewers basic info
  • Max 50–60 characters

What’s in a namestrap?

  • First and last name
  • Function
  • Affiliation (company, organization, institution)

What’s not in a namestrap?

  • The guest’s entire resume
  • Every institution the guest is currently affiliated with
  • Irrelevant former institutional affiliations
  • Academic titles
  • Honorary titles

No academic titles

  • Unless he/she is a medical doctor, then Dr.
  • Avoid: Ph.D., dr. hab., prof, etc.

No honorary titles

  • Avoid: Sir, Lady, etc.

Avoid “Head of X”

  • Find out what the actual title is
  • Often: Director of X, Name of Institution

Choose One Institution

  • Whatever is most relevant to the interview

Abbreviated Institutions

  • Don’t just give the abbreviated name of an unfamiliar institution

OK

UN, NATO, EU

Not OK

NASK, MEI, OSW, PISM

  • NASK Research and Academic Computer Network
  • Middle East Institute
  • Centre for Eastern Studies
  • Polish Institute of International Affairs

Function and Institution

  • The institution alone is (usually) not enough
  • If no function is known, or there is no specific function, then omit the function

Not OK:

Function of the Institution

Function at the Institution

OK:

Function, Institution

Govt. Ministers

  • List the country and position
  • If more than one position, choose the relevant one
  • Avoid “Secretary of State” – they’re usually deputy ministers

Examples

Adam Bodnar

Polish Justice Minister

Elina Valtonen

Finnish Foreign Minister

Vivian Motzfeldt

Greenlandic Minister of Foreign Affairs and Independence

Ambassadors

  • X Ambassador to Y
  • Polish Ambassador to Germany
  • U.S. Ambassador to Poland

Examples

Maciej Hunia

Polish Ambassador to Israel

Bridget Brink

U.S. Ambassador to Ukraine

Lawmakers

  • List the country, position, and party name (in English, if available)

Examples

Eero Heinäluoma

Finnish MEP, Socialists and Democrats

Anna Maria Żukowska

Polish MP, The Left

Kira Rudik

Ukrainian MP, Voice Party

Military

  • Use military titles if guest is speaking as a defense expert or in a military capacity

Examples

Gen. Ben Hodges (Ret.)

Former Commander, U.S. Army Europe

Gen. Oleksandr Syrskyi

Commander in Chief, Armed Forces of Ukraine

Diacritical Characters

Spelling

  • Latin alphabet: Use original spelling (including “special” characters)

Cyrillic Alphabet

  • Use official transcription
  • Avoid “Polish” spelling of Ukrainian/Russian names

Exercises

Yusuf Can

Coordinator for the Middle East Program (MEP) at the Wilson Center

Yusuf Can

Middle East Program Coordinator, Wilson Center

Director of Turkish Program, MEI
